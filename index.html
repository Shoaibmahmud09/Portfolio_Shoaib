Introduction

About Me

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Industrial and Production Engineering at the Military Institute of Science and Technology (MIST), Dhaka, where I also completed my B.Sc. in Industrial and Production Engineering with a CGPA of 3.77/4.00 and ranked 4th among 51 graduates.

My research focuses on operations research and mixed-integer programming, prescriptive analytics for infrastructure resilience and disaster response, graph neural networks for network vulnerability and retrofit prioritization, and decision-making under uncertainty.

Most of my current infrastructure-focused work has been developed through the Risk-Informed Systems Engineering (RISE) Lab under the supervision of Dr. Himadri Sen Gupta, alongside my role as a Research Associate with Colorado State University Pueblo.

I also teach, supervise undergraduate thesis students, and mentor capstone projects, while remaining open to funded PhD opportunities aligned with resilient infrastructure and data-driven decision support.

What I Work On

Research Focus

Infrastructure Resilience

Assessing vulnerability and optimizing recovery of transportation networks, power grids, and urban infrastructure following natural disasters.

Graph Neural Networks

Applying GNNs to capture network topology for seismic vulnerability assessment and retrofit prioritization of infrastructure systems.

Prescriptive Analytics

Developing pipelines that go beyond risk prediction to generate actionable response strategies for urban flood and disaster scenarios.

Operations Research

Mixed-integer programming and robust optimization for supply chain design, logistics network planning, and resource allocation.

Humanitarian Logistics

Equity-aware dispatch and resource allocation models for post-disaster emergency response and community recovery.

Supply Chain Resilience

Reverse logistics network design and optimization for semiconductor supply chains facing disruption and shortage.

Network Optimization

Modeling and solving network-level decision problems for transportation, logistics, and infrastructure systems using mathematical programming.

Mixed-Integer Programming

Formulating and solving large-scale combinatorial optimization problems using MILP and IBM ILOG CPLEX for real-world engineering applications.

Thesis: Reverse Logistics Network Design for Semiconductor Supply Chain

Developed a mixed-integer linear program (MILP) integrating forward and reverse logistics to mitigate semiconductor chip shortages. Formulated and solved using IBM ILOG CPLEX. Designed a quick-response reverse logistics network to recover, test, and redeploy functional chips, reducing new facility requirements and shortening delivery lead times.
